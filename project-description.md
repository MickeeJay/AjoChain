# AjoChain Project Description

## Problem

In West and East Africa, informal savings circles remain one of the most trusted ways for people to save and access lump sums. Market traders, transport workers, artisans, and salaried workers alike rely on Ajo, Susu, Chama, Tontine, and similar rotating schemes because they fit local cash flow and social trust. The problem is that these groups still depend on manual collection, notebook tracking, and one organizer holding the money, which creates disputes, missed payments, and the risk that a single person can compromise the entire group.

## Solution

AjoChain turns that familiar savings pattern into a MiniPay Mini App on Celo. Instead of a human treasurer, smart contracts define the group rules and enforce them on-chain: who belongs to the group, how much each member contributes, when each round runs, and when the pooled funds can be released. This removes the single point of failure while keeping the social model of rotating savings intact.

The product is designed to feel native to the way people already save. Members can join from their phone, contribute in cUSD, and follow a clear on-chain record of payments, payouts, pauses, and exits. Because the system is built for MiniPay, the experience stays lightweight and mobile-first, which matters for users who want a familiar savings tool without the overhead of a full crypto onboarding flow.

## Target Users

- Market traders who need disciplined weekly or daily savings.
- Gig workers who want flexible but structured contribution rounds.
- Diaspora savers who support family or participate in home-country savings groups.
- Community savings circles in Nigeria, Kenya, and Ghana.

## Why Blockchain Is Necessary

Blockchain is not just a cosmetic upgrade here. The core problem in rotating savings is trust, coordination, and rule enforcement across people who may not know each other equally well. A blockchain contract gives every participant the same shared state, the same rules, and the same audit trail. That makes contribution history, payout order, and completion status transparent and harder to dispute. Without blockchain, the same logic still depends on one person or one server to keep the truth.

## Why Celo Specifically

Celo fits this use case because it is built for mobile-first financial activity. Phone-number identity makes onboarding more approachable for everyday users. Sub-cent transaction fees matter for frequent savings contributions, especially when users may be paying small amounts many times per month. Celo's MiniPay distribution also gives AjoChain a direct path to users already inside a stablecoin-ready wallet experience.

## Why MiniPay Specifically

MiniPay is the right entry point because it already reaches 14M users, supports stablecoin workflows, and reduces onboarding friction. Users do not need to learn a separate crypto wallet flow before they can participate. That matters for rotating savings, where the goal is to make the app feel like a practical money tool, not a speculative blockchain demo.

## Competitor Analysis

| Option | Strengths | Weaknesses | AjoChain Advantage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Traditional Ajo | Familiar, social trust, simple | Manual tracking, organizer risk, limited transparency | Keeps the tradition with transparent on-chain rules and records |
| Tanda | Community-based savings logic | Often localized, manual, and organizer-dependent | Provides a programmable and verifiable version of the same model |
| WhatsApp groups | Easy communication and coordination | No native payments, no enforcement, no state consistency | Adds actual financial rails and contract enforcement |
| AjoChain | Transparent, rule-based, mobile-first | Requires blockchain support | Built specifically for MiniPay and cUSD flows |

## Business Model

AjoChain can earn revenue through a few aligned paths:

- Savings completion credential NFT: an on-chain soulbound badge proving successful participation and completion of a savings round.
- Group insurance: a protection layer that can cover missed contributions or group defaults through a partnered product.
- Premium tier: advanced analytics, better reminders, historical insights, and group administration tools for power users or organizers.

## Traction Plan For April

The near-term target is to validate the product with real usage rather than only demos.

- Onboard 2 to 3 real savings groups in Nigeria, Kenya, or Ghana.
- Drive at least 50 mainnet transactions across group creation, joins, and contributions.
- Collect feedback on trust, ease of use, and payout clarity.
- Use that feedback to improve onboarding, messaging, and the MiniPay experience.
